# B2B LinkedIn Content Playbook

## Core Content Pillars

### 1. Contrarian Insight

Challenge common industry assumptions.

Examples:
- Brand matters more than attribution.
- AI visibility matters more than rankings.
- Tools don't create outcomes, strategy does.

Experts:
- Dave Gerhardt
- Chris Walker
- Amanda Natividad

---

### 2. Frameworks

Break complex problems into actionable steps.

Examples:
- Marketing strategy from scratch
- Category creation process
- ABM execution model

Experts:
- Amanda Natividad
- April Dunford

---

### 3. Systems & Workflows

Show repeatable processes.

Examples:
- Signal-to-Pipeline workflow
- GTM tool stack
- LinkedIn outreach system

Experts:
- Maja Voje
- Ross Simmonds

---

### 4. Data-Driven Insights

Use research to explain market shifts.

Examples:
- Zero-click search
- AI visibility trends
- Audience behavior changes

Experts:
- Amanda Natividad
- Ross Simmonds
- Hiten Shah

---

### 5. Founder & Operator Lessons

Personal experiences transformed into business lessons.

Examples:
- Leaving corporate careers
- Building a category
- Scaling GTM teams

Experts:
- Justin Welsh
- Jason Lemkin

# Common Post Structure

## Pattern 1: Contrarian

Hook
↓
Challenge conventional wisdom
↓
Evidence
↓
New perspective
↓
Question

Example:
"Most marketers are optimizing for the wrong internet."

---

## Pattern 2: Framework

Problem
↓
Framework
↓
Step-by-step explanation
↓
Action

Example:
"How would you start a marketing strategy from scratch?"

---

## Pattern 3: Data → Insight

Statistic
↓
Interpretation
↓
Business implication
↓
Recommendation

Example:
"68% of searches end without a click."

---

# Cross-Expert Observations

Across all analyzed experts:

1. Strong opinions outperform neutral commentary.
2. Frameworks outperform generic advice.
3. Market shifts are recurring content themes.
4. Personal stories are used to support expertise.
5. Data is used to validate arguments.
6. Most posts focus on one core idea only.
7. Simplicity beats completeness.
