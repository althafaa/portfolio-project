## Title: Build a Business That Serves Your Life
## URL: https://youtu.be/dZx610FbOBc?si=8XAtmZ9VlnIz_wsX

**Key Insights:**
**1. Bootstrapping can be a viable path for B2B SaaS**
Not every SaaS company needs venture capital. Some can start with services, consulting, paid pilots, or a niche workflow to build cash flow before scaling.

**2. Funding changes founder incentives**
Venture capital can help a SaaS company grow faster, but it can also create pressure to chase rapid growth, larger markets, and investor outcomes. Bootstrapping gives founders more control over product, customers, and company direction.

**3. Strong demand should not require constant convincing**
If a SaaS company has to repeatedly convince buyers to care, the issue may be weak positioning, unclear ICP, or low market awareness. The best-fit customers should feel naturally aligned with the problem and solution.
