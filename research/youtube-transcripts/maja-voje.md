## Title: Build a Simple Go-To-Market Strategy
## URL: https://youtu.be/zL7MrP2nxVY?si=z0yvw2cwSnmYz_SG

**Key Insights:**
**1. AI startups need a focused GTM system, not just a product**
Maja’s GTM Power Hour framework breaks strategy into six parts:
- Market Understanding
- Early Customer Profile
- Product
- Pricing
- Positioning
- Growth Motions

This is useful because many AI products fail not because the technology is weak, but because the market, customer, pricing, and growth motion are unclear.

**2. Early-stage GTM should be scrappy and relationship-driven**
For early AI startups, the best first customers may come from:
- personal networks
- partners
- agencies
- pilots
- quick wins
- free channels

The goal is not to build a scalable machine immediately. The goal is to learn fast, validate assumptions, and find repeatable customer patterns.

**3. Partnerships can be a strong growth motion for AI products**
In the Echoin example, Maja highlights partners or agencies as an Early Customer Profile because they already have access to the target market. This can help AI startups scale trust and distribution faster than selling directly from zero.
