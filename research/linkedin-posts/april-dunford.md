**Summary:** 
April Dunford explains that when a market changes significantly, such as with AI, companies need to communicate a clear point of view about the future of the market. ## Post 4
**Date:**  
February 2026

**Link:** 
https://www.linkedin.com/posts/aprildunford_you-dont-want-to-start-any-positioning-work-activity-7422666982032056322-QbQr

**Summary:** 
April Dunford explains that teams should not begin positioning work until they make several key decisions: whether now is the right time, who the positioning is for, what exactly is being positioned, and which persona the positioning should target.

**Key Takeaway:** 
Positioning work should begin only after the team agrees on timing, audience, positioning object, and target persona.

**Theme:**
Positioning Readiness, B2B SaaS Positioning, Go-To-Market Strategy, Customer Understanding, Persona Alignment, Team Alignment

**Content Framework:**
Before positioning:
1. Decide if now is the right time.
2. Decide who the positioning is for.
3. Decide what exactly is being positioned.
4. Decide which persona values it.

If skipped:
↓
Internal confusion
↓
Mid-process debate
↓
Rework
↓
Weak positioning

**Why This Post Is Valuable:**
1. Strong fit for B2B SaaS positioning and go-to-market strategy.
2. Provides a practical checklist before starting positioning work.
3. Useful for LinkedIn organic strategy because content needs a clear audience and positioning foundation.
4. Reinforces that customer understanding should come before final positioning.
